# Cybercrime Case Study: UPI Payment Fraud

UPI payment fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes affecting students and young users in India. It usually happens when a fraudster tricks a person into sharing sensitive banking information or approving a payment request without realizing it.

A common scenario begins when the victim receives a phone call, WhatsApp message, or SMS claiming that they have won a prize, need to verify a bank account, or must receive a refund. The scammer then sends a fake payment link or asks the victim to scan a QR code. Many people mistakenly believe scanning a QR code means receiving money, but in reality, it often authorizes payment from their own account.

College students are frequent targets because they use UPI apps regularly for food, shopping, travel, and splitting bills. If the fraud is successful, the victim may lose money instantly and may also feel panic, embarrassment, or fear.

This type of cybercrime spreads quickly because scammers sound convincing and create urgency. It shows the importance of awareness, careful verification, and safe digital habits.
